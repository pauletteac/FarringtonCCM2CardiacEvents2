ℹ️ Diagnosis: Takotsubo Cardiomyopathy

"Broken heart syndrome" mimics ACS but coronary arteries are normal. Key features:

  • Typically affects postmenopausal women
  • Triggered by intense emotional or physical stress
  • Modest troponin elevation (lower than expected for wall motion abnormality)
  • Elevated BNP disproportionate to troponin
  • Generally reversible with supportive care

Hospital Course

ℹ️ Takotsubo Management

Requires supportive care, not aggressive ACS therapy:

  • Beta-blockers to reduce catecholamine effects
  • ACE inhibitors for LV remodeling
  • Monitor for heart failure, LV thrombus, arrhythmias
  • Anticoagulation only if LV thrombus develops
  • Most patients recover LV function within weeks

Key Takeaways

📋 Summary

Troponin elevation is common in ICU patients but does not always indicate ACS.

Classification of Troponin Elevation

  • Type 1 MI: Plaque rupture with coronary thrombosis — ACS protocol and revascularization
  • Type 2 MI: Supply-demand mismatch — treat underlying cause
  • Non-ischemic injury: Myocarditis, PE, Takotsubo, blunt cardiac injury — etiology-specific management

Clinical Pearls

  • "Treat the patient, not the troponin" — clinical context is essential
  • Avoid unnecessary anticoagulation in critically ill patients
  • EKG interpretation and troponin trends are diagnostically important
  • Use imaging selectively to guide diagnosis
  • Consult cardiology when the diagnosis is unclear
